Hello everyone LeBron Raymone James Sr. is the one of the most valuable player of ALL-TIME.

We all know he is the four time NBA champion (2012,2013,2016,2020), four time NBA Finals Most Valuable Player (2012,2013,2016,2020), four time NBA Most Valuable Player of the year (2009,2010,2012,2013).

Lebron's career statistics

In Cavaliers (2003-2010)

In 2003 he drafted as the first overall pick of the 2003 NBA draft his hometown Cleveland Cavaliers and he achieved the first Rookie of the year from Cavs. His regular season games final he averages of 20.9 points, 5.5 rebounds, and 5.9 assists per game, he also became the third player in league history to average at least 20 points, 5 rebounds, and 5 assists per game as a rookie. 2008-2010 is his MVP seasons as a cavaliers. But he leaved Cleveland Cavaliers and he joined Miami Heats. Cleveland Vavaliers.

In Miami Heat (2010-2014)

He officially signed with the Miami heat on July 10, 2010, through a sign-and-trade-deal which sent two second-and two first round draft picks to the Cavaliers and gave the team the option to swap first round picks with the Heat in 2012. In Heat Chris Bosh and Dwyane Wade were his best career partners.James has 2 time NBA champions from Miami Heat with two time NBA finals Most Valuable Player. For the Finals, James averaged 28.2 points, 7.8 rebounds, and 2.0 steals per game in Miami Heat.

Return to Cavaliers (2014-2018)

On June 25, 2014, James opted out of his contract with the Heat, and on July 1 he officially became an unrestricted free agent. On July 12 he signed with the Cavaliers again. A month after James's signing, the Cavs acquired Kevin Love from the Minnesota Timberwolves, forming a new star trio along with Kyrie Irving. In 2016 NBA final to begin the series Cavs fell behiand 3-1 against Golden State Warriors, including two blowout losses. Next 4 games Cavs came back and win the NBA champion including James responded back-to-back 41 point games in 5th and 6th game and 7th game Lebron posted trible-double and made a number of key plays, including a chasedown block on Andre Iguodala's go ahead layup attempt. This is the first and only one franchise history of the NBA came back from 3-1 series on final.
